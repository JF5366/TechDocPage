Introduction

Raising and showing dairy cattle is a highly rewarding expereince that can build a lifetime of memories and also maximize the sale price of your herd.

This article will help you pick the best possible cattle to win a competition, properly prepare those animals, and to perform well in the ring. Showing dairy cattle is highly rewarding for dairy farmers and newcomers alike.

There is a lot that goes in to dairy cattle competitions, much more than meets the eye. The first step is to select a breed. Holsteins, the ones with black and white spots, are the most popuar, and the hardest competitions to win. Some other breeds like Jersey and Guernsey are less popular and will have less competition in the ring.

Popular Breeds:

  • Holstein
  • Jersey
  • Guernsey
  • Brown Swiss
  • Red & White
  • Ayrshire
  • Milking Shorthorn

The next consideration when showing cattle is the age categories. There are usually many categories split up based on age:

  • Spring, Winter and Fall Heifer Calves
  • Summer,Spring, Winter and Fall Yearling Heifer
  • Yearling Heifer in Milk
  • Summer Junior Two-Year-Old Cow
  • Junior and Senior Two-Year-Old Cow
  • Junior and Senior Three-Year-Old Cow
  • Four-Year-Old Cow
  • Five-Year-Old Cow
  • Six-Year-Old Cow and Over

Once you have decided on your breed and age you would like to show, next you should learn how to pick the best cattle to win.

Genetic Selection of Show Cattle

When you pick your first now, you should be looking for the same criteria that the judge will be looking for in the ring, but more on that later.

After you shop around for cattle and start to build a high-yield genetic herd, you will need to select the best sires to breed with your heifers. You should look for sires with the desirable traits that your animals may be lacking.

At Home Preparation

Preparing cattle to win a competition begins long before you step in the ring. You want to provide the best possible veterinary care, housing and food quality to optimize your cattle's potential.

It is also very important to work with your cattle from a young age so that they are comfortable around people and walking on a lead. Show Procedures

Traveling to and staying at competition locations can be very complicated and costly. Many farmers stay in RVs at the fairgrounds, others stay in nearby hotels. If the show is a long distance from home, it is best to coordinate with local dairy farms to send your coes together to cut down on costs.

You will need to keep your cattle clean for the duration of the fair, so it is important to wash your cattle daily. You will also need to clip their long winter coat, leaving fur on the topline. Before the show you need to use a blow dryer to get the topline hair to stand up in a perfect line. Hair stray can keep the topline in place, and help to fluff the tail.

Don't forget your show whites!

Judging Criteria

There are 4 main judging categories, and many criteria within each:

  1. Frame (15%): Rump, Front End, Back/Loin, Stature and Breed Characteristics.
  2. Dairy Strength (25%): Ribs, Chest, Thighs, Neck, Withers and Skin.
  3. Rear Feet and Legs (20%): Movement, Read Legs - Side View, Read Legs - Read View, Feet, Thurl Position, Hocks, Bone and Pasterns.
  4. Udder (40%): Udder Depth, Read Udder, Udder Cleft, Fore Udder, Teats and Udder Balance and Texture.

Judges will examine animals and compare them to breed standards for each of these categories, scoring them. Keep in mind each show is just one person's opinion, so you may score higher or lower than you were expecting.
